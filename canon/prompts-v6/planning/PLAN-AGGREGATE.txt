## PLAN-AGGREGATE — Final (LLM-only, chunk-first, no rigid JSON)

### Purpose

Consolidate planning artifacts into a single **corridor spine** that stays readable, checkable, and portable as a Target for progress and outcome checks. This is **planning clarity**, not an implementation plan.

---

## Input Grammar (UI layer)

Aggregate **[Planning Inputs : Targets]** into a corridor for **[Corridor Purpose : Intent]**.
If present, use **[Recency Rule : Intent]** to choose which planning version(s) are active, and use **[Corridor Strictness : Intent]** to decide how tight the corridor should feel.

Required blanks: **Planning Inputs**, **Corridor Purpose**.
Optional blanks: **Recency Rule**, **Corridor Strictness**.

---

## Output Contract (chunk-first, hard)

Output an **Aggregate Plan Pack** as TEXT chunks with the following exact titles:

Required:

* **Corridor Spine** — a compact corridor statement that ties together aim, done, fence, and tripwires in readable prose.

Optional (include only when clearly supported by the planning inputs):

* **North Star** — the active outcome and success signal.
* **Done Receipts** — observable completion receipts phrased as “done when …”.
* **Scope Fence** — what is in-scope and explicitly out-of-scope.
* **Tripwires** — early alarms with a short response posture.
* **Conflict Ledger** — only if planning inputs contradict or duplicate; describe conflicts without inventing a resolution.
* **Assumptions** — only if needed; each line begins with `Assumed:`.

Hard rules:

* Do not invent new requirements or new corridors; only consolidate what exists.
* Do not output tasks, steps, or patch plans.
* Do not ask questions. If planning inputs are incomplete, produce the smallest runnable corridor and record assumptions.

---

## Recommended Chunk Beat Guidance (for writing quality, not required output)

Corridor Spine should read like a short “corridor vow”: what we’re aiming for, what counts as done, what we refuse to take on, and what would alarm us early. Done Receipts should be concrete enough that a progress check can map evidence to them later. Scope Fence should feel like a refusal boundary, not a wish list.

---
