You are executing the action SF-01: Lay the Ground.
Your job is to seed the canon foundation for this game’s supernatural world in a way that can guide both story growth and software meaning later. This is not a lore dump. It is a small set of stable pillars that future moves can safely build on.

This action produces one core outcome: a compact canon seed made of a few world pillars that feel inevitable and reusable.

INPUTS YOU MAY USE
- user_intent: what kind of world or vibe the player wants, and what it should support.
- context (read-only): may include <summary>, <projection_slice …>, and recent <story>/<software> additions. Treat <summary> as the best compressed truth.
- targets: optional, up to 3 target bundles.

  - <projection_slice kind="INDEX"> may be present, but you do not need it for this move.

PILLAR DISCIPLINE
Choose a small number of pillars and make each one stable enough to be referenced later. A pillar can be a world law, a cost, a taboo, a scarce resource, a recurring constraint, a factional pressure, or a metaphysical boundary. Each pillar must constrain the world in a way that creates future consequences without needing more explanation.

WHAT TO PRODUCE
Create a canon seed in chunks of tight prose. Each chunk must be immediately legible as a collectible card. Write in a vivid, playable voice, not an academic one.
Include:
- A short statement of the world’s baseline reality and the “supernatural exception” that bends it.
- A small set of canon pillars expressed as compact, declarative prose that can be quoted later as rules of the world.
- One sentence that explains what kind of stories this canon naturally produces, without listing plots or proposing next steps.

CONSTRAINTS (HARD)
- Do not output code.
- No academic tone.
- Do not use bullets, numbering, or checklist formatting in your chunk bodies. If you are defining multiple pillars, unwrap them into tight prose using sentence boundaries and light separators.
- Do not reprint large context or target bodies. Compress and distill.
- Do not invent contradictions with existing canon. If existing canon is unclear, prefer being minimal rather than adding complexity.

MISSING INPUT BEHAVIOR
