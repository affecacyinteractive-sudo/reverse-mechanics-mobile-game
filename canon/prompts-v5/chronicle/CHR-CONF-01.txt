## CHR-CONF-01 — Chronicle Placement Confirm (Where it Landed in the Journal)

### Input Grammar

**Simple prose (2 required blanks)**
Confirm where **[Keystone Pack : Targets]** appears in **[Chronicle Window : Chronicle]**.

**Required blanks (2)**

* **[Keystone Pack : Targets]** — the keystone/run artifact you want to locate in the journal.
* **[Chronicle Window : Chronicle]** — the chronicle entries window (or store slice) to search within.

**Pro prose (optional boosters)**
Confirm where **[Keystone Pack : Targets]** appears in **[Chronicle Window : Chronicle]**, using **[Index Anchors : Targets]** and constrained by **[Confirmation Focus : Intent]**.

**Optional blanks (descriptions)**

* **[Index Anchors : Targets]** — handles/anchors that should match between keystone and chronicle.
* **[Confirmation Focus : Intent]** — what to emphasize (exact match line, surrounding context, anchors/tags).

---

### What to Produce

Produce a **Chronicle Placement Confirm Pack** (TEXT chunks) that makes the journal insertion explicit and scannable.

Across the pack, include:

1. **Placement Summary (TEXT)** — 1–3 lines stating what keystone was searched for and what window was used.
2. **Matched Entry (TEXT)** — the chronicle entry line that corresponds to the keystone:

   * include `school`, `journal_line`, and `anchors/tags` if present.
3. **Neighborhood Context (TEXT, optional)** — 2–6 lines showing:

   * the immediately previous and next entries (or nearest neighbors) so placement is legible in the journey flow.
4. **Match Basis (TEXT)** — 1–3 lines explaining why this entry matches (shared run_id, shared keystone_ref, anchor overlap, or exact journal line match).
5. **Closure Check (TEXT)** — one sentence stating what is now confirmed (“this run’s keystone is recorded in the journal and can be recalled/filtered”).

Hard guards:

* Do not invent matches; if no match exists in the provided window, output a single chunk:

  * Title: `Placement Not Found`
  * Body: one sentence stating no matching entry was found in this window (no questions).
* No action IDs and no “next move” routing.

---

### Recommended Chunk Beat Categories (with 1-line descriptions)

* **Placement Summary** — what was searched and where.
* **Matched Entry** — the exact journal line/entry that represents the keystone.
* **Neighborhood Context (optional)** — nearby entries for journey legibility.
* **Match Basis** — why this match is correct (run/keystone/anchors).
* **Closure Check** — confirms insertion is real and recallable.
